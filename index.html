🛰️ Satellites 7 MARKS

Definition & Working Principle

  • Object that revolves around a planet under gravitational force
  • Launched with horizontal velocity so gravity = centripetal force → stable orbit
  • Communication: Earth → Uplink → Satellite transponder → Downlink → Earth

Types by Orbit

  • LEO — 200–2000 km, 90–120 min period, Earth observation
  • MEO — 2000–35786 km, GPS/Navigation
  • GEO — 35786 km, fixed over one point, TV & weather

Types by Function

  • Communication — TV, Radio, Internet, Mobile
  • Weather — Cyclone, rainfall, climate
  • Navigation — GPS, NavIC
  • Remote Sensing — Agriculture, land use
  • Scientific — Space research, astronomy
  • Military — Surveillance, defence

Components of Satellite

  • Payload — Mission equipment (transponders, cameras, atomic clocks, telescopes)
  • Power System — Solar panels + Battery + Power control unit
  • Attitude & Orbit Control — Gyroscope, reaction wheels, thrusters
  • Propulsion — Fuel tanks, thrusters, valves
  • Thermal System — Insulation, heat pipe, radiator
  • Telemetry & Tracking — Send/receive commands, position feedback
🚀 PSLV 10 MARKS7 MARKS

Definition

  • 4-stage, medium-lift launch vehicle developed & operated by ISRO
  • India's most reliable rocket — alternates Solid → Liquid → Solid → Liquid
  • Payload: 1750 kg to SSO, 3800 kg to LEO

Key Features

  • 4-stage alternating solid/liquid configuration
  • Highest success rate in India
  • Multi-orbit capability (LEO, SSO, GTO)
  • World record: 104 satellites in one mission (PSLV-C37)

Variants

  • PSLV-G — Standard (6 strap-ons)
  • PSLV-XL — Extended large boosters
  • PSLV-CA — Core alone (no boosters)
  • PSLV-DL — Two boosters

Achievements & Applications

  • Launched Chandrayaan-1 and Mangalyaan
  • Record: 104 satellites in a single mission (PSLV-C37)
  • Earth observation, remote sensing, weather monitoring, navigation, scientific missions
🚀 GSLV & LVM3 7 MARKS

GSLV

  • 3-stage heavy lift by ISRO for GEO orbit (35786 km)
  • Stage 1 — Solid core (HTPB), 4 liquid strap-ons
  • Stage 2 — Liquid Vikas engine (UDMH + N₂O₄)
  • Stage 3 — Cryogenic (LH₂ + LOX), very high efficiency
  • Variants: Mk-I (Russian) → Mk-II (indigenous cryo) → Mk-III = LVM3

LVM3 (Launch Vehicle Mark-3)

  • India's heaviest rocket — 4–5 tonne satellite capacity
  • Stage 0 — Solid strap-ons (S200)
  • Stage 1 — Core liquid (L110)
  • Stage 2 — Cryogenic upper stage (C25)
  • Supports Gaganyaan human spaceflight
  • Missions: Chandrayaan-2, 3; commercial launches
🔥 Rocket Propulsion 10 MARKS7 MARKS

Definition & Principle

  • Method by which a rocket produces thrust by expelling mass at high velocity
  • Based on Newton's 3rd Law — every action has equal & opposite reaction
T = ṁVe + (Pe − Pa) × Ae
  • ṁ = mass flow rate | Ve = exhaust velocity | Pe/Pa = exit/ambient pressure | Ae = nozzle exit area

Components of Rocket Engine

  • Propellant system — Fuel + Oxidizer (e.g. LH₂ + LOX)
  • Combustion chamber — Chemical → Thermal energy
  • Nozzle — Accelerates gases to supersonic velocity; thermal → kinetic energy
  • Turbopumps — Feed propellants at high pressure (liquid engines)
  • Control system — Directs thrust for steering

Solid vs Liquid vs Hybrid

FeatureSolidLiquidHybrid
PropellantPre-mixed solidSeparate liquidSolid + Liquid oxidizer
Throttle❌✅⚡ Partial
Restart❌✅⚡ Partial
ComplexitySimpleComplexMedium
EfficiencyLowHighModerate
ExamplePSLV S1, SRBFalcon 9, GSLVSpaceShipTwo

Solid ✅ Adv

  • Simple design, quick ignition
  • High thrust-to-weight ratio
  • Long storage life

❌ Disadv

  • Cannot shut down once ignited
  • Difficult to control thrust
  • Lower efficiency than liquid

Liquid ✅ Adv

  • Throttle, restart, shut-off possible
  • Higher efficiency
  • Suitable for reusable systems

❌ Disadv

  • Complex plumbing & turbopumps
  • Higher cost, risk of leaks
  • More ground support needed
🛸 Introduction to UAV

Definition

  • UAV = Unmanned Aerial Vehicle — aircraft without a human pilot onboard
  • Controlled: (a) Remotely by human operator OR (b) Autonomously via onboard computer + pre-programmed flight plans
  • Uses aerodynamic forces; can carry sensors, cameras, payloads. Also called Drones.
📐 Types of UAVs 7 MARKS

By Design

  • Fixed Wing — Long endurance, like airplane, needs runway
  • Rotary — VTOL, quadcopter-style, hovers
  • Hybrid — Combines both (e.g. delivery drones)

By Operation

  • Remote — Controlled by ground operator
  • Autonomous — Pre-programmed path
  • Semi-autonomous — Combination of both

By Size

  • Nano — Very small
  • Micro — up to 250 g
  • Mini — up to 2 kg
  • Small — 0.5–25 kg
  • Medium — up to 150 kg
  • Large — Military, 150+ kg

Key Components

  • Flight Controller — Brain; processes sensors, maintains stability
  • Propulsion — Motors + Propellers
  • Sensors — GPS, radar, cameras, multispectral
  • Autonomy System — AI path planning, obstacle avoidance
  • Battery — Power source

⚙️ Multirotor Drone Assembly 10 MARKS

Definition

  • Integrating mechanical, electrical, and control components to build a stable flying system

8 Key Steps

  • 1. Frame Assembly — Structural frame (X, H, or + config) to support all components
  • 2. Motor Installation — Brushless DC motors on arms; CW or CCW orientation for stability
  • 3. ESC Connection — Electronic Speed Controllers regulate each motor speed per flight controller signals
  • 4. Flight Controller Setup — CPU installed; uses gyroscope & accelerometer to stabilise the drone
  • 5. Power Distribution — PDB connects battery to ESCs & all electronics
  • 6. Propeller Attachment — Propellers fixed on motors to generate lift and thrust
  • 7. Battery & Receiver — Battery powers drone; radio receiver enables communication with transmitter
  • 8. Calibration & Testing — Sensors and ESCs calibrated before test flights
📜 Drone Regulation in India 7 MARKS

Regulatory Body: DGCA (Directorate General of Civil Aviation)

  • Drone Rules 2021 — Digital Sky platform
  • Registration — Unique ID required for most drones
  • NPNT — No Permission, No Take Off — digital auth required before every flight
  • Airspace Zones — 🟢 Green (no perm, limited height) / 🟡 Yellow (perm needed) / 🔴 Red (no-fly)
  • Altitude limit — Max 400 ft (120 m)
  • VLOS — Drone must remain visible to operator at all times
  • Penalties — ₹25,000 to ₹5,00,000 fine + possible imprisonment

Drone Categories & Licence Requirements

  • Nano <250 g — No licence required
  • Micro 250 g–2 kg — No licence for non-commercial
  • Small 2–25 kg — Licence required
  • Medium/Large 25 kg+ — Licence required

Unit 1 — 2-Mark Q&A
Q: What is a satellite? ▾
A satellite is an object that revolves around a planet under gravitational force. Artificial satellites are launched for communication, navigation, Earth observation, and scientific purposes.
Q: What is GEO orbit? ▾
Geostationary Earth Orbit (GEO) is at 35,786 km altitude. A satellite here remains fixed over one point on Earth and is used for TV broadcasting and weather monitoring.
Q: What is rocket propulsion? ▾
Rocket propulsion is the method by which a rocket produces thrust by expelling mass at high velocity, based on Newton's 3rd Law. The basic thrust equation is: T = ṁVe + (Pe − Pa)Ae.
Q: What is PSLV? ▾
PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) is a 4-stage, medium-lift rocket by ISRO that alternates solid and liquid propulsion stages. It holds the world record of launching 104 satellites in a single mission (PSLV-C37).
Q: What did Chandrayaan-3 achieve? ▾
Chandrayaan-3 (2023) achieved India's first successful soft landing near the Moon's south pole, making India the first country to land there. The Pragyan rover confirmed the presence of sulphur on the lunar surface.
Q: What is LEO? ▾
Low Earth Orbit (LEO) is at 200–2000 km altitude with an orbital period of 90–120 minutes. It is used for Earth observation satellites, the ISS, and imaging missions.
Unit 2 — 2-Mark Q&A
Q: Define UAV / Drone ▾
An Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) is an aircraft that operates without a human pilot onboard. It is controlled either remotely by a ground operator or autonomously through onboard computers and pre-programmed flight plans.
Q: What is NPNT? ▾
NPNT (No Permission, No Take Off) is India's drone policy under Drone Rules 2021. A drone operator must obtain digital authorisation via the Digital Sky platform before flying — without approval, the drone cannot take off.
Q: What is a Flight Controller in a drone? ▾
A flight controller is the brain of the drone that processes inputs from gyroscopes and accelerometers to maintain stability. It sends signals to ESCs to regulate motor speeds and control altitude, tilt, and movement.
Q: What is VLOS? ▾
VLOS (Visual Line of Sight) requires the drone to remain visible to the operator during flight without optical aids. It is a key safety requirement under India's Drone Rules 2021.
Q: Two UAV applications in agriculture ▾
1. Monitoring crop health using multispectral sensors to detect disease or nutrient stress. 2. Precision pesticide and fertilizer spraying over large fields with minimal waste.
Q: What is an ESC in a drone? ▾
An Electronic Speed Controller (ESC) regulates each motor's rotational speed based on signals from the flight controller. It converts DC battery power into the correct current for each motor.
